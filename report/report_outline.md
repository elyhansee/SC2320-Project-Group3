# Spatial-Behavioural Analytics: Mapping Nutritional Vulnerability and Food Accessibility in Singapore's Aging Districts

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Motivation

Singapore is one of the fastest ageing societies in Asia. According to the Department of Statistics, roughly one in four residents will be aged 65 or above by 2030. Elderly residents depend on three things at once in ways that the general population does not. They need walkable access to affordable prepared food such as hawker centres and wet markets. They need frequent and accessible public transport for daily errands. They also benefit from barrier free building infrastructure, which reduces the physical effort required to move through the built environment.

Most public indicators treat these dimensions in isolation. One agency maps hawker centres, another maps bus stops, and a third describes building accessibility. The compounding effect, where two or three of these barriers coincide within the same neighbourhood, is precisely the situation that can quietly turn an otherwise food secure area into a high risk one for its older residents. This project addresses that gap at the subzone level using only publicly available data.
